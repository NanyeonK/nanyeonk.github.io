Glossary

Key terms and definitions for real estate tokenization

Security Token

A digital asset that represents ownership in a real-world asset and is subject to securities regulations.

Read more

STO (Security Token Offering)

The process of issuing security tokens to investors, analogous to an IPO but using blockchain-based tokens.

Read more

Fractionalization

Dividing ownership of an asset into smaller units (tokens) to lower minimum investment thresholds.

ERC-1400

An Ethereum token standard designed for security tokens, supporting partitioned balances and transfer restrictions.

ERC-3643

An Ethereum token standard (also known as T-REX) for compliant security tokens with on-chain identity verification.

REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust)

A pooled investment vehicle that holds real estate assets and trades on public exchanges, required to distribute 90% of taxable income.

Read more

SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle)

A legal entity created to hold a specific asset, commonly used to structure tokenized real estate offerings.

Read more

ATS (Alternative Trading System)

A SEC-regulated trading venue for securities that is not a national exchange, used for secondary trading of security tokens.

Reg D

SEC regulation providing exemptions from registration for private placements, commonly used for STO issuances to accredited investors.

Reg S

SEC regulation providing exemptions for offerings made outside the United States to non-US investors.

Reg A+

SEC regulation allowing issuers to raise up to $75 million from both accredited and non-accredited investors with simplified reporting.

Read more

MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets)

EU regulation establishing a comprehensive framework for crypto-asset service providers and issuers.

KYC/AML

Know Your Customer and Anti-Money Laundering procedures required for investor verification in securities offerings.

Smart Contract

Self-executing code on a blockchain that enforces the rules of a token, including transfer restrictions and compliance checks.

Howey Test

The legal test from SEC v. W.J. Howey Co. (1946) used to determine whether an instrument qualifies as a security under US law.

Read more

NAV (Net Asset Value)

The total value of a fund's or token's underlying assets minus liabilities, used to assess whether tokens trade at a premium or discount.

Liquidity Premium

The additional return investors demand for holding an asset that is difficult to sell quickly at fair value.
